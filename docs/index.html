About

Hello! I'm Khaled, a Computer Science lecturer and researcher developing human-centered, trustworthy AI. I pair machine learning with large language and vision-language models and explainable AI, and I evaluate these systems with rigorous human-centered methods.

The aim is to make real-world decision-support and interactive systems more reliable, interpretable, and usable. My interests run across adaptive user interfaces, AI for health, and edge intelligence. I finished my B.Sc. in CSE at MIST and joined as a Lecturer in April 2024, where I teach and supervise undergraduate research and capstone theses, several of which have led to peer-reviewed publications.

Research

Human-Centered, Trustworthy AI

My research connects strong models with the people who rely on them. I care about systems that are accurate, but also clear, safe, and accountable across adaptive interfaces, health, and edge settings.

Ongoing Research

  • Trustworthy & Explainable Skin-Lesion Classification. Extending a hybrid DINO-ViT and quantum-annealing optimization pipeline on HAM10000 toward a clinician-in-the-loop tool that adds uncertainty quantification, visual explanations, and fairness across skin tones.
  • Human-Centered Evaluation & Reliability of Vision-Language Models. Studying when and why users over- or under-trust multimodal LLM outputs, and designing interface and calibration strategies for dependable, accessibility-focused decision support.
  • Air Quality & Airborne-Disease Burden Prediction. Modeling the link between air pollution and airborne diseases to forecast disease burden using machine learning over sensor and clinical data.
  • Technical vs Non-Technical User Interaction with LLMs. A human-centered study of how user expertise shapes prompting behavior, interaction patterns, and outcomes across conversational LLM assistants.
